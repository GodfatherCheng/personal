Work in Progress

"Overseas Investment and Supply Chain Integration: Evidence from Chinese Mining Industry"

This paper investigates how initial foreign direct investments in the mining sector influence subsequent market entry and trade flows from upstream firms of the same origin country. The study focuses on two key aspects: (1) the gateway effect of pioneer investments on related suppliers and its variation across different types of goods and services in the supply chain, and (2) the mechanisms through which initial investments reduce entry barriers, particularly examining the roles of information frictions, relationship-specific investments, and vertical relationships. Using evidence from the Chinese mining industry, this research aims to provide insights into the dynamics of international supply chain integration and the spillover effects of foreign direct investment.
